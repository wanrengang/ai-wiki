# I²B-LPO: Breaking Reasoning Homogenization in RLVR

**Paper**: ACL 2026 Main | Source: 阿里达摩院 - 智能决策团队

## Overview

I²B-LPO addresses the **exploration-exploitation trade-off** in Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR). Current rollout sampling naturally favors exploitation—models quickly converge to few high-probability templates, generating semantically homogeneous trajectories despite surface-level differences.

> **Core Problem**: Blindly increasing sampling doesn't lead to more efficient exploration because trajectories share similar underlying logic, weakening reward differences and advantage signals.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. High-Entropy Nodes = Critical Decision Points

- Strategy entropy correlates with logical turns and self-correction behaviors
- At high-entropy positions, different decoding strategies show amplified performance differences
- Low-entropy positions don't show significant differences

### 2. Reasoning Length ≠ Effective Reasoning

Standard GRPO training shows:
- Accuracy plateaus early
- Response length and 4-gram repetition rate continue rising

This indicates models generate longer, repetitive content rather than more effective reasoning.

### 3. Two Key Problems in Existing Entropy-Based Methods

| Problem | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Reward Hacking** | Model generates meaningless divergence (lengthy, repetitive, off-topic) to game entropy rewards |
| **Inductive Bias** | Token-level perturbations only change surface expressions, can't break pre-trained reasoning preferences |

## Core Innovation: I²B-LPO Framework

### Two Key Mechanisms

#### 1. Entropy-Driven Latent Variable Branching

**Process**: For each initial rollout `r = (o₁, ..., oₜ)`:

```
Calculate policy entropy at each position:
    Hₜ = -Σ p(token) × log p(token)

Select high-entropy positions as branching points:
    Branch if Hₜ > τ (high percentile threshold)
```

**Pseudo Self-Attention (PSA) Injection**:
```python
# Modulate RMSNorm scaling with latent variables
scaled_params = latent_variable × γ(t)  # γ(t) decays over generation

# Map latent variables to additional K/V, concatenate to original attention
final_attention = Original_Attention + PSA_Attention
```

PSA acts as "implicit thought hints" that continuously influence subsequent reasoning trajectories.

#### 2. Information Bottleneck Self-Reward

**Quality Score**:
```
Score = I(r; a) - β × I(q; r)

Where:
- I(r; a) = information contribution of trajectory to final answer
- I(q; r) = constraint to prevent redundancy
```

**Filtering**: Keep Top-N trajectories with highest IB scores for GRPO policy updates.

## Experimental Results

### Training Data
- **6,486** MATH samples
- **13,583** DAPO samples

### Benchmarks
- AIME2025 / AIME2024
- MATH-500
- OlympiadBench
- GSM8K

### Key Results

| Metric | Improvement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Accuracy** | Up to +5.3% |
| **Semantic Diversity** | Up to +7.4% |

I²B-LPO maintains stable high entropy levels in later training stages, preventing premature convergence to single reasoning templates.

---

## Low IB Score Trajectory Problems

1. **Empty Verbosity**: Excessive "Let me think" or "It is important to note" padding
2. **Repetitive Loops**: Restating problems without new reasoning
3. **Logical Drift**: Correct expression but wrong formulas or derivation directions

High IB score trajectories are shorter, more direct, and each step serves the final answer.

## Summary

I²B-LPO shifts RLVR exploration from "repeated sampling" to **"generating more discriminative reasoning trajectories at key nodes"**:

| Mechanism | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| High-Entropy Branching | Generate diverse trajectories at points of genuine uncertainty |
| Information Bottleneck Self-Reward | Filter concise, high-information, answer-focused paths |

Results demonstrate I²B-LPO achieves higher accuracy and diversity within limited sampling budgets across multiple math reasoning benchmarks.
